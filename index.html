VitroScript Working Paper Series

Execution Economics Canon

A doctrine of institutional execution examining why capital, policy, and design intent consistently fail to translate into delivered outcomes, and what structural execution systems are required to correct this failure.

Core Proposition

Most development failure is not a resource constraint. It is an execution architecture failure: the structural gap between decision and delivery within institutional systems.

Three Core Architectures

Execution Architecture

How decisions fail to become outcomes through delays, fragmentation, and weak accountability structures between approval and delivery.

Value Architecture

How value is generated, lost, or never captured across production systems, supply chains, and institutional coordination layers.

Performance Architecture

How institutions fail to become investable and delivery-capable systems with predictable and measurable execution capacity.

Featured Insight

The Coordination Tax

Execution failure is rarely caused by lack of resources. It is produced by repeated alignment cycles across fragmented institutional actors, creating a structural cost that compounds across time and weakens delivery capacity.
